About This Website

From Here to There: A Navajo’s Guide to Higher Education was created to support Indigenous students—especially Navajo students—as they explore, prepare for, and navigate the college journey. This site offers clear guidance, helpful resources, and culturally grounded support to empower students at every step.

Our Purpose

To provide Indigenous learners with accessible, culturally aligned information about higher education pathways—written with our communities in mind.

Our Goals

  • Demystify the college application and enrollment process.
  • Offer practical tips and timelines for preparation.
  • Highlight resources tailored to Indigenous students.
  • Celebrate our cultural strengths and resilience.
  • Build confidence and clarity around higher education choices.

Meet Your Auntie

Ya’ahteeh,

Shí éí Nika yinishyé. Tá’áchʼíní nishłį́, Tsénahabiłníí bashishchiin.

Welcome, and thank you for being here. My name is Nika, and I am of the Red Running Into the Water people, born for the Sleeping Rock people.

Growing up, college felt like something I only saw in movies—study montages, dorm rooms, and then suddenly a graduation. I knew college existed, but I didn’t know how I would exist in it. No one in my family had gone, and I didn’t have anyone to ask. It wasn’t until a supportive teacher helped me with applications, fee waivers, and essays that I realized higher education could be within reach.

But even after I was accepted, I didn’t know what to do next. Deadlines passed, and I quietly stepped away because I felt overwhelmed and unsure of where to begin. Eventually, someone encouraged me to try community college. So I did—one class at a time, whenever I could afford it. I learned how to navigate the campus, how to ask questions, and how to persevere.

Fourteen years later, I earned my associate’s degree. Today, I’m a senior at ASU working toward my bachelor’s, a first-generation student, and an auntie who helps guide my nieces and nephews on their own paths. Now, I hope to help you with yours.

If you grew up on the reservation, you know what it means to be resilient, resourceful, and determined. We come from a nation within a nation, and understanding the world outside our borders helps us move forward without being left behind.

I hope this website provides you with clarity, comfort, and confidence as you begin your journey. Ahéhee’ for visiting.
